    MRI Scan

    What is MRI Scan?

    MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) is a non-invasive diagnostic imaging technique that uses powerful magnets and radio waves to produce detailed images of the body's internal structures. It is commonly used to evaluate the brain, spinal cord, joints, and other soft tissues to help detect abnormalities, injuries, or diseases.

    Types of MRI Scans

    Brain MRI
    • Detects brain tumors, strokes, infections
    • Evaluates causes of headaches and seizures
    • Checks multiple sclerosis or brain injuries
    • Monitors brain structure and blood flow
    Spine MRI
    • Detects slipped discs or spinal cord compression
    • Evaluates spinal tumors or infections
    • Identifies causes of back pain or weakness
    • Assesses congenital spine abnormalities
    Joint & Musculoskeletal MRI
    • Evaluates ligament and tendon injuries
    • Detects cartilage damage and arthritis
    • Diagnoses muscle tears
    • Assesses sports injuries
    Cardiac MRI
    • Examines heart structure and function
    • Detects heart muscle damage
    • Assesses congenital heart disease
    • Evaluates blood flow and valve function
    Abdominal & Pelvic MRI
    • Detects liver and kidney disorders
    • Evaluates tumors and infections
    • Diagnoses uterine and prostate conditions
    • Stages abdominal cancers
    Fetal MRI
    • Provides detailed fetal imaging
    • Detects congenital abnormalities
    • Complements ultrasound findings
    • Helps plan neonatal care

    Why Choose MRI Scan

    MRI scans provide high-resolution images without using harmful radiation. They are ideal for identifying soft tissue conditions and neurological disorders, making them essential for accurate diagnosis.

    Why This Test

    MRI helps detect internal conditions that may not be visible on X-rays or ultrasound, supporting early diagnosis and effective treatment planning.

    CT Scan

    What is CT Scan?

    CT (Computed Tomography) scan is an advanced imaging technique that combines multiple X-ray images to create detailed cross-sectional pictures of the body. It is commonly used to evaluate bones, organs, blood vessels, and soft tissues for injuries, infections, or diseases.

    Types of CT Scans

    Head CT
    • Detects brain injuries, bleeding, and swelling
    • Evaluates strokes and head trauma
    • Identifies brain tumors or structural abnormalities
    • Checks sinus and skull bone conditions
    Chest CT Scan
    • Detects lung infections, pneumonia, and tuberculosis
    • Identifies lung cancer and pulmonary nodules
    • Evaluates heart, lungs, and major blood vessels
    • Assesses chest injuries or congenital abnormalities
    Abdominal & Pelvic CT Scan
    • Detects tumors, cysts, and infections in abdominal organs
    • Evaluates liver, kidney, pancreas, and spleen disorders
    • Helps diagnose appendicitis, gallstones, or intestinal issues
    • Assesses uterus, ovaries, and prostate abnormalities
    CT Angiography
    • Examines arteries and veins in detail
    • Detects aneurysms, clots, and vascular blockages
    • Evaluates blood flow in brain, chest, abdomen, and legs
    • Helps plan vascular surgeries or stent placement
    Whole Body CT Scan
    • Used for cancer screening and early detection
    • Detects injuries after major trauma
    • Evaluates spread of cancer
    • Used for preventive imaging
    Spine CT Scan
    • Detects spinal fractures
    • Evaluates herniated discs
    • Identifies tumors or infections
    • Assists surgical planning
    Sinus CT Scan
    • Detects sinus infections
    • Evaluates nasal blockages
    • Helps plan surgery
    • Assesses chronic sinusitis

    Why Choose CT Scan

    CT scans provide precise and detailed images, making them highly effective for diagnosing internal conditions quickly. They are essential for trauma evaluation, detecting tumors, planning surgeries, and guiding treatment decisions.

    Why This Test

    CT imaging helps detect and monitor medical conditions not visible on standard X-rays, supporting accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.

    Ultrasound Scan

    What is Ultrasound Scan?

    Ultrasound scan is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of organs and tissues inside the body. It is widely used to monitor pregnancy, assess abdominal organs, detect cysts, and evaluate blood flow and soft tissue structures.

    Types of Ultrasound Scans

    Abdominal Ultrasound
    • Evaluates liver, gallbladder, pancreas, kidneys, and spleen
    • Detects gallstones, liver disease, and kidney stones
    • Identifies cysts, tumors, or fluid collections
    • Helps diagnose abdominal pain
    Pelvic Ultrasound
    • Examines uterus, ovaries, bladder
    • Detects fibroids and ovarian cysts
    • Evaluates pelvic pain or abnormal bleeding
    • Helps assess infertility issues
    Obstetric (Pregnancy) Ultrasound
    • Monitors fetal growth and development
    • Detects congenital abnormalities
    • Checks placenta and amniotic fluid
    • Determines due date and multiple pregnancies
    Breast Ultrasound
    • Detects breast lumps and cysts
    • Differentiates solid vs fluid-filled masses
    • Guides biopsy procedures
    • Used along with mammography
    Thyroid Ultrasound
    • Evaluates thyroid size and structure
    • Detects nodules or enlargement
    • Guides FNAC biopsy
    • Monitors thyroid disorders
    Doppler (Vascular) Ultrasound
    • Measures blood flow in arteries and veins
    • Detects clots and blockages
    • Diagnoses varicose veins and DVT
    • Evaluates carotid and limb circulation
    Renal (Kidney) Ultrasound
    • Examines kidneys and urinary bladder
    • Detects stones, cysts, infections
    • Evaluates urinary obstruction
    • Monitors chronic kidney disease
    Prostate Ultrasound (TRUS)
    • Evaluates prostate size and structure
    • Detects enlargement or tumors
    • Guides prostate biopsy
    • Used in male infertility evaluation

    Why Choose Ultrasound Scan

    Ultrasound scans are safe, painless, and radiation-free. They provide real-time imaging and are ideal for evaluating organs, guiding procedures, and monitoring health conditions.

    Why This Test

    Ultrasound helps in early detection of abnormalities and supports accurate diagnosis and treatment planning, especially for pregnancy and abdominal organs.

    PET Scan

    What is PET Scan?

    PET (Positron Emission Tomography) Scan is an advanced imaging technique that helps detect changes in cellular function and metabolism in the body. It is commonly used to identify cancer, monitor treatment progress, and evaluate brain and heart conditions.

    Types of PET Scans

    Oncology PET Scan
    • Detects cancers and tumors at an early stage
    • Determines if tumors are benign or malignant
    • Helps in cancer staging
    • Monitors chemotherapy or radiation response
    Cardiology PET Scan
    • Evaluates blood flow to heart muscles
    • Detects reduced oxygen supply
    • Identifies coronary artery disease
    • Guides cardiac treatment planning
    Neurology PET Scan
    • Evaluates brain metabolism and activity
    • Detects Alzheimer‚Äôs and dementia
    • Helps diagnose epilepsy and Parkinson‚Äôs
    • Assesses brain tumors and strokes
    Whole Body PET Scan
    • Provides complete metabolic assessment
    • Detects cancer spread across organs
    • Used for early cancer detection
    • Supports preventive health evaluation
    PET-MRI Scan
    • Combines PET metabolic data with MRI detail
    • Ideal for neurological and oncological cases
    • Provides superior soft tissue contrast
    • Lower radiation than PET-CT
    PET-CT Scan
    • Combines PET functional imaging with CT anatomy
    • Highly accurate cancer diagnosis
    • Used for radiation therapy planning
    • Detects small and early lesions

    Why Choose PET Scan

    PET scans detect metabolic changes before structural damage occurs, enabling early diagnosis, accurate staging, and effective treatment monitoring.

    Why This Test

    PET scans help detect cancer, neurological disorders, and cardiac conditions early, guiding precise medical decision-making and therapy evaluation.

    X-Ray

    What is X-Ray?

    X-Ray is a quick and non-invasive imaging technique used to view the structure of bones and certain internal organs. It helps in detecting fractures, infections, or abnormalities in the skeletal system and chest area.

    Types of X-Rays

    Chest X-Ray
    • Detects lung infections such as pneumonia or tuberculosis
    • Evaluates heart size and chest wall structures
    • Identifies fractures of ribs or chest bones
    • Helps monitor chronic lung conditions like asthma or COPD
    Dental X-Ray
    • Detects cavities and tooth decay not visible in routine exams
    • Evaluates jawbone and root health
    • Helps in planning dental implants and braces
    • Identifies cysts, abscesses, and impacted teeth
    Bone X-Ray
    • Detects bone fractures and dislocations
    • Evaluates arthritis and joint degeneration
    • Checks for bone infections and tumors
    • Monitors bone healing after treatment
    Abdominal X-Ray
    • Detects intestinal blockages and perforations
    • Identifies swallowed foreign objects
    • Helps diagnose kidney stones and gallstones
    • Evaluates stomach and bowel conditions in emergencies
    Mammography (Breast X-Ray)
    • Used for early detection of breast cancer
    • Identifies lumps, tumors, and calcifications
    • Distinguishes between benign and suspicious growths
    • Guides further imaging tests and biopsies

    Why Choose X-Ray

    X-Ray scans are a fundamental diagnostic tool for evaluating bone health, lung conditions, and other internal structures. They are fast, safe, and provide valuable insights for early detection and monitoring of medical conditions.

    Why This Test

    X-Ray allows doctors to quickly identify fractures, dislocations, infections, or abnormalities. It is particularly useful for assessing chest infections, bone injuries, and joint issues.

    DEXA Scan (Bone Densitometry)

    What is a DEXA Scan?

    A DEXA Scan (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry) is a specialized imaging test that measures bone mineral density (BMD). It is used to diagnose osteoporosis, assess fracture risk, and monitor changes in bone health over time.

    Types of DEXA Scans

    Central DEXA Scan
    • Measures bone density in the spine and hip (most common method)
    • Used for diagnosing osteoporosis and predicting fracture risk
    • Provides detailed and highly accurate results
    Peripheral DEXA Scan
    • Measures bone density in smaller bones such as wrist, heel, or finger
    • Often used for quick screenings
    • Less detailed compared to central DEXA scan

    Why Choose a DEXA Scan

    DEXA scans are highly accurate and considered the gold standard for measuring bone density. Early detection of bone loss allows timely interventions, reducing the risk of fractures and improving overall bone health.

    Why This Test

    The DEXA Scan helps identify low bone density before symptoms appear.
