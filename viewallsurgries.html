    Common Fractures Requiring Surgery

    Explore some of the most frequently encountered fractures that may need surgical intervention. Each section provides a concise, reader-friendly overview ‚Äî causes, treatment options, recovery time, and rehabilitation tips ‚Äî helping you understand what to expect during recovery.

    Distal Radius Fracture

    A fracture near the wrist joint caused commonly by a fall on an outstretched hand. It‚Äôs one of the most frequent upper-limb injuries, especially among elderly patients. Treatment options range from simple casting to open reduction and internal fixation (ORIF) using plates and screws. Early wrist physiotherapy is crucial to regain flexibility and strength within 6‚Äì8 weeks.

    Femur Fracture

    The femur, or thigh bone, is the body‚Äôs longest and strongest bone. Femur fractures typically occur due to high-energy trauma such as road accidents. Surgical fixation using intramedullary nails or plates is often required. Recovery usually takes 3‚Äì6 months, with structured physiotherapy to restore balance, walking stability, and muscle strength.

    Tibial Fracture

    The tibia (shin bone) often breaks due to direct impact or sports injuries. Depending on the type, doctors may recommend casting, intramedullary nailing, or plate fixation. Weight-bearing is gradually resumed under supervision, and full recovery generally occurs in 10‚Äì12 weeks.

    Humerus Fracture

    The upper-arm bone may fracture near the shoulder, mid-shaft, or elbow. Mild fractures may heal with a brace, while displaced ones require ORIF surgery. Gentle, early physiotherapy prevents stiffness and ensures complete shoulder mobility in 8‚Äì10 weeks.

    Ankle Fracture (Bimalleolar / Trimalleolar)

    Twisting or impact injuries often cause complex ankle fractures. Surgical fixation restores alignment and stability. Early ankle exercises under guidance help restore flexibility. Most patients return to full mobility within 10‚Äì12 weeks.

    Clavicle (Collarbone) Fracture

    Caused by falls, sports injuries, or direct shoulder impact. While many heal with a sling, displaced fractures may need surgical plating. Recovery takes 2‚Äì3 months, with most regaining full shoulder function after physiotherapy.

    Hip Fracture (Neck / Intertrochanteric)

    Common in older adults due to osteoporosis or falls. Surgery is essential, often with partial or total hip replacement. Rehabilitation begins within days, focusing on walking and balance training for a 4‚Äì6 month recovery.

    Patella (Kneecap) Fracture

    Typically results from a direct fall or impact on the knee. Treatment depends on displacement ‚Äî from conservative bracing to ORIF surgery. Early movement exercises help prevent stiffness, with recovery in 8‚Äì12 weeks.

    Olecranon (Elbow Tip) Fracture

    Often caused by falling on a bent elbow. Surgery (using plates or tension-band wiring) is common. Early movement is encouraged to avoid stiffness, with recovery usually complete within 6‚Äì8 weeks.

    Pelvic Fracture (Unstable Type)

    A high-impact trauma injury requiring immediate stabilization. Surgery may involve internal or external fixators. Long-term rehab helps restore walking and core stability, with recovery spanning several months.

    Common Orthopedic Surgeries

    These are among the most frequently performed orthopedic procedures that help restore joint function, correct deformities, and relieve pain. Each surgery aims to help patients regain mobility and improve quality of life.

    Knee Replacement (Total / Partial Arthroplasty)

    Knee replacement surgery replaces the damaged joint surface with an artificial implant. It is highly effective in relieving chronic arthritis pain and restoring movement. Patients typically begin walking with support within 24‚Äì48 hours and resume normal activities within 8‚Äì12 weeks.

    Spinal Surgery (Discectomy, Laminectomy, Fusion, etc.)

    Performed to relieve pressure on spinal nerves or stabilize the spine. Procedures include removing herniated discs, decompressing nerve roots, or fusing unstable segments. Physiotherapy focuses on posture correction and back strengthening.

    Ankle Repair / ORIF (Open Reduction and Internal Fixation)

    In complex ankle fractures, bones are surgically realigned and fixed using metal plates or screws. Early physiotherapy ensures a stable, mobile joint, preventing long-term stiffness or arthritis.

    ACL Reconstruction

    This procedure repairs the anterior cruciate ligament in the knee using a tendon graft. It‚Äôs common among athletes and active individuals. Early movement exercises and gradual weight-bearing promote full recovery in 4‚Äì6 months.

    Hip Replacement (Total / Hemi Arthroplasty)

    Hip replacement surgery relieves severe joint pain due to arthritis or fractures. Artificial implants replicate natural joint motion, enabling patients to walk comfortably again. Full rehabilitation takes 3‚Äì6 months.

    Shoulder Arthroscopy / Rotator Cuff Repair

    A minimally invasive procedure to treat shoulder injuries like rotator cuff tears and impingement. It involves tiny incisions and offers faster recovery with minimal scarring and improved shoulder motion.

    Fracture Fixation (Plating, Nailing, External Fixator)

    Surgical stabilization of fractured bones using metal implants or external fixators. These methods allow early limb movement and faster bone healing with reduced deformity risks.

    Meniscus Repair / Meniscectomy

    Arthroscopic procedure for treating torn knee cartilage. Depending on tear type, the meniscus is repaired or partially removed. Rehabilitation focuses on restoring knee motion and preventing future injuries.

    Carpal Tunnel Release

    This procedure releases pressure on the median nerve in the wrist, resolving tingling and numbness. Patients regain hand strength within a few weeks and return to work soon after recovery.

    Arthroscopic Debridement / Joint Cleaning

    Minimally invasive surgery to remove damaged tissue or bone fragments from joints like the knee or shoulder. It improves mobility, reduces inflammation, and delays joint degeneration.

    Common Onco (Cancer) Surgeries

    These are the most frequently performed oncological procedures designed to remove cancerous tissue, preserve organ function, and improve survival outcomes. Each surgery is planned carefully for both medical and aesthetic recovery.

    Breast Cancer Surgery (Lumpectomy / Mastectomy)

    Depending on the stage, the tumor alone or the entire breast may be removed. Breast-conserving surgeries and reconstruction techniques now help achieve excellent cosmetic and therapeutic outcomes. Recovery time is usually 2‚Äì4 weeks with gradual physiotherapy.

    Uterus Cancer Surgery (Hysterectomy ¬± Lymph Node Dissection)

    Removal of the uterus, often with surrounding lymph nodes, helps treat endometrial and cervical cancers. The procedure may be open, laparoscopic, or robotic. Recovery is typically within 4‚Äì6 weeks.

    Ovarian Cancer Surgery (Oophorectomy / Debulking)

    Involves removing one or both ovaries and affected tissues to minimize tumor load. Often combined with chemotherapy for optimal outcomes. Recovery focuses on nutrition and hormonal support.

    Abdominal Tumor Surgery (GI / Retroperitoneal Resection)

    Tumors in the digestive tract or abdomen are surgically removed with precision to preserve healthy organs. Postoperative care includes nutrition support and gradual resumption of digestive function.

    Colorectal Cancer Surgery (Colectomy / Rectal Resection)

    Removal of cancerous parts of the colon or rectum, followed by reconnection of the bowel. Minimally invasive techniques reduce pain and allow early discharge in 4‚Äì5 days.

    Head and Neck Cancer Surgery (Laryngectomy / Mandibulectomy, etc.)

    Complex reconstructive surgeries restore appearance and function after tumor removal. Modern microsurgical flaps and laser excision techniques enhance outcomes with shorter recovery.

    Lung Cancer Surgery (Lobectomy / Pneumonectomy)

    Removal of one lung segment (lobectomy) or entire lung (pneumonectomy) to eliminate cancer. Post-surgery breathing exercises and rehab improve lung capacity and overall recovery.

    Prostate Cancer Surgery (Radical Prostatectomy)

    Complete removal of the prostate gland using minimally invasive or robotic techniques. Patients regain continence and sexual function with proper rehabilitation over 3‚Äì6 months.

    Thyroid Cancer Surgery (Total / Partial Thyroidectomy)

    Removal of the thyroid gland or its affected part helps cure early-stage thyroid cancer. Lifelong hormone therapy ensures normal metabolism and energy balance.

    Bone and Soft Tissue Sarcoma Surgery (Wide Excision / Limb Salvage)

    Involves removing bone or soft tissue tumors while preserving limb function whenever possible. Advanced reconstruction and prosthetic implants allow excellent long-term mobility.

    Common Surgical and Diagnostic Procedures

    These are essential surgical and diagnostic procedures performed routinely across medical specialties. Each aims to treat common health conditions, detect diseases early, and promote faster patient recovery.

    Piles (Hemorrhoids Surgery)

    A minimally invasive procedure to remove or shrink swollen veins in the anal region that cause pain, bleeding, and discomfort. Depending on severity, treatment may involve laser surgery, stapler hemorrhoidectomy, or band ligation. Most patients recover quickly, resuming normal activities within a few days.

    Fistula (Anal Fistula Surgery)

    Performed to treat abnormal tunnels connecting the anal canal and skin. Surgical methods include fistulotomy, laser ablation, or seton placement to prevent recurrence and promote healing. The procedure offers long-term relief from discharge, pain, and infection, with minimal downtime.

    Laparoscopic Appendectomy

    Minimally invasive removal of an inflamed appendix using small keyhole incisions. It allows quicker recovery, less scarring, and minimal postoperative pain‚Äîpatients typically resume normal activity within 5‚Äì7 days.

    Hernia Surgery (Inguinal / Umbilical / Ventral)

    The weakened abdominal wall is repaired either through open or laparoscopic mesh placement. Laparoscopic methods provide faster healing, reduced discomfort, and lower recurrence rates.

    Cholecystectomy (Gallbladder Removal)

    Surgical removal of the gallbladder to treat stones or infection. Most are performed laparoscopically, allowing discharge within 24 hours and full recovery in 1‚Äì2 weeks.

    Endoscopy (Upper GI / Colonoscopy)

    A diagnostic procedure using a flexible camera to view the digestive tract. It helps detect ulcers, bleeding, or tumors and can include biopsy sampling for lab analysis.

    Biopsy Procedures (Breast, Liver, Lymph Node, etc.)

    Tissue sampling using needle or minor surgical methods to diagnose infections, tumors, or cancer. Usually done under local anesthesia with minimal downtime.

    Cataract Surgery (Phacoemulsification / IOL Implantation)

    Clouded lenses are replaced with clear artificial intraocular lenses (IOLs). This painless outpatient procedure restores vision within days and allows quick return to normal activities.

    Tonsillectomy / Adenoidectomy

    Removal of enlarged tonsils or adenoids to treat recurrent throat infections and breathing difficulties. Performed under general anesthesia, with complete recovery in 7‚Äì10 days.

    Cyst / Abscess Excision or Drainage

    A minor procedure to remove or drain infected cysts or abscesses from the skin or soft tissue. Usually done under local anesthesia with rapid wound healing and minimal scarring.

    Common Obstetrics and Gynecology (OBG) Procedures

    These gynecological and obstetric procedures support women‚Äôs reproductive health, fertility, and safe delivery. Each treatment is performed with precision and care to ensure the best outcomes for every woman.

    D&C (Dilation and Curettage)

    A short procedure to remove tissue from the uterus, usually performed after miscarriage or to diagnose uterine conditions. It‚Äôs quick, safe, and recovery happens within a few hours.

    Tubectomy (Female Sterilization)

    A permanent method of birth control involving blocking or sealing the fallopian tubes. Usually performed laparoscopically, with same-day discharge and minimal discomfort.

    Cervical Stitch (Cervical Cerclage)

    A preventive surgery for pregnant women at risk of preterm birth. The cervix is reinforced with a stitch to help maintain pregnancy safely until term.

    Recanalization (Tubal Reversal)

    A microsurgical procedure to reopen blocked fallopian tubes after tubectomy, restoring fertility potential. Success depends on the tube‚Äôs condition and length.

    Hysterectomy (Abdominal / Vaginal / Laparoscopic)

    Surgical removal of the uterus for fibroids, heavy bleeding, or cancer prevention. Minimally invasive laparoscopic methods ensure faster healing and reduced hospital stay.

    Cesarean Section (LSCS)

    Performed to safely deliver a baby when vaginal delivery is not advisable. Modern anesthesia and gentle techniques allow quick recovery and minimal scarring.

    Ovarian Cystectomy

    Laparoscopic removal of cysts from the ovary while preserving healthy ovarian tissue. Suitable for treating benign cysts, endometriomas, or dermoid cysts.

    Hysteroscopy (Diagnostic / Operative)

    A thin telescope-like camera is used to examine and treat the inside of the uterus. It helps remove small fibroids, polyps, or adhesions with minimal pain and downtime.

    Myomectomy (Fibroid Removal)

    Surgery to remove uterine fibroids while preserving fertility. Can be done via open, laparoscopic, or hysteroscopic approach depending on fibroid size and number.

    Pap Smear / Colposcopy

    A screening test for cervical cancer where cells are gently collected for analysis. Colposcopy provides a magnified view of the cervix for early detection and management.
